Q: what is the main idea behind Otsu’s thresholding method?
A: A Otsu's thresholding method is a technique used to automatically determine the optimal threshold value that maximizes the difference between the intensities of foreground and background pixels in an image. This is achieved by calculating the cumulative distribution function of pixel intensities across the entire image and then calculating the threshold value that minimizes the variance among the probabilities of pixels. The process iteratively adjusts the threshold until this maximum occurs, effectively separates the objects from background noise. However, this method can be computationally intensive for high-class variance, particularly when dealing with complex images containing significant variations in illumination and texture.
